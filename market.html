Culver City Market & Rents: What It Costs, What It Rents, What Pencils

Live comps & rents, July 2026 · each build strategy priced out to a rent roll and a yield · a scoping analysis, not an appraisal · Conscious Communities Real Estate

CHOOSE A PLAYBOOK: Single-Family · R1 (SB9 + ADU) Multifamily · R2 & up Market & Rents

This ties the two build playbooks to real money: what each property type actually sells for, what's on the market, what you'd buy for, and the rents each unit throws off — then prices out every strategy to a rent roll and a yield. Culver City is a high-cost Westside submarket: SFRs run ~$1.6M, small income buildings ~$375k–1M a door, and rents from ~$2,150 (studio) to ~$8,950 (4BR house).

The punchline. For a hold, the best rent-to-acquisition is buy an existing fourplex and add ADUs (~11.6% gross yield, ~$316k all-in per door) — you buy income that already cash-flows near a 5% cap, then each rent-control-exempt ADU drives the per-door cost down. The lower-capital, simplest version of the same thesis is an R2 duplex + 2 ADUs (~9.6% gross, ~$388k/door, ~$1.55M all-in). SB9 and ground-up multifamily are build-to-sell / merchant plays — they don't cash-flow as holds on a $1.6M land basis.

5. How to read these numbers

  • Scoping, not an appraisal. Commission a feasibility/zoning study and get contractor bids before acting. Not personalized investment advice.
  • Retrieved vs estimated. All sale/list prices, $/sf, DOM, comps, and rent medians are live scrapes (Redfin, Zillow, Realtor.com, PropertyShark, LoopNet, Crexi; Zillow Rental Manager, Rentometer, Zumper, RentCafe). All build costs are estimates (~$400–550/sf ground-up; ADUs ~$150k conversion to ~$350k detached), and the ADU rent (~$2,800) has no ADU-specific source.
  • "Appreciation" is noise. The +27% / +34% YoY median-sale jumps are mix-shift on ~64–74 sales/month, not real appreciation — Zillow's index shows ~1.4% YoY. Treat true appreciation as ~1–5%.
  • Blended vs single-family. The $717/sf and $1.31M "typical value" blend condos in and understate R1 — use house-only ~$1,000–1,100/sf and ~$1.6M for SFR.
  • Jurisdiction. Many 5–20 unit comps sit in Palms (90034) / Mar Vista (90066) = City of LA, not Culver City — different zoning and rent control. In-city stock is thin.
  • Unit counts assume ADU feasibility. The 9-unit fourplex play assumes 5 ADUs fit the lot; de-rate to fourplex + 2–3 ADUs if not. Land prices are asking (no closed comps); expect to pay at/above ask in a thin market.
