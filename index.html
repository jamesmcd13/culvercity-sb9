SB9 + ADU Rules and Strategy: Adding Units on a Culver City R1 Lot

City of Culver City · no lot split · what you can build, how big each unit can be, and two ways to a four-unit project · prepared by Conscious Communities Real Estate · July 2026

On a single R1 lot in the City of Culver City, with no lot split, you can build a four-unit project two ways. State law (SB9) forces the city to permit two primary dwellings on an R1 lot even though the base zone allows only one, and state ADU law adds two more. The whole game is floor area: Culver City's R1 zone is a flat 0.45 FAR (lot area × 0.45), the two SB9 dwellings draw on that budget, and the ADUs stack on top — 800 sf per ADU FAR-free, guaranteed.

The one FAR rule to remember — it's all-or-nothing, never partial. An ADU is FAR-free only when it qualifies as a state “66323” exemption unit, and then the whole unit is free — 800 sf for a detached ADU on a single-family lot. Build it one square foot larger and it is no longer an exemption unit: the entire ADU counts toward the 0.45 FAR (not just the amount over 800). There is no “first 800 sf free” credit in Culver City. The 800 sf is a buildability floor (state law bars the City from using FAR to deny an 800 sf ADU — GC 66321(b)(3)), not a discount on a bigger one. No detached ADU may ever exceed 1,200 sf (GC 66314(d)(5)).
Strategy 1
Duplex in front + two detached ADUs in back
  • 2 units at full R1 height (the duplex, 27–30 ft) sharing the 0.45 FAR budget
  • The duplex makes the lot multifamily, so two detached back ADUs are whole-unit FAR-free under the multifamily exemption — 800 sf each bankable, larger on the upside — and can rise to up to 30 ft
  • Best for maximum total square footage — two fully-free detached units
Strategy 2
Two detached main dwellings + attached ADU + detached ADU
  • 3 units at full R1 height (two houses + the attached ADU); one detached ADU at 16–20 ft
  • Only the detached 800 sf ADU is FAR-free; the attached ADU is not an exemption unit, so it counts toward the budget
  • Best for the two main houses as the biggest, most sellable product

2. How big can each unit be — and how FAR counts it

Your floor-area budget is lot area × 0.45 — flat across all R1 lots in Culver City (a 6,000 sf lot = 2,700 sf; a 7,500 sf lot = 3,375 sf; a 10,000 sf lot = 4,500 sf). The two SB9 dwellings share that budget. The one way to add floor area on top of it is a state-exemption ADU — and that exemption is all-or-nothing per unit.

How an ADU escapes FAR: as a whole exemption unit. A detached ADU is FAR-free only when it qualifies as a GC 66323 statewide-exemption unit, and then the entire unit is off the budget. The qualifying size is 800 sf on a single-family lot (GC 66323(a)(2)); on a duplex / multifamily lot the detached-ADU exemption has no 800 sf statutory cap (GC 66323(a)(4)) — bankable at 800 sf, larger on the upside. That larger multifamily exemption is Strategy 1's whole advantage.
Go over the exemption and the WHOLE unit counts — not just the overage. On a single-family lot, growing a detached ADU from 800 sf to 1,200 sf does not put only the extra 400 sf on the FAR budget — it puts all 1,200 sf there, because the unit is no longer a 66323 exemption unit and reverts to the R1 standards including FAR (CCMC 17.400.095.N.1; HCD ADU Handbook, Mar 2026). There is no “first 800 sf free” credit in Culver City — that partial rule exists only in cities that expressly wrote it in (e.g., Los Gatos). The 800 sf is a buildability floor: FAR can never be used to deny you an 800 sf detached ADU, but it is not a discount on a larger one.

3. State ADU height: 16, 18, 20 — or 30 ft in Culver City

A detached ADU's height ladder comes from GC 66321(b)(4):

  • 16 ft — the base height.
  • 18 ft — if the lot is within a half-mile walking distance of a major transit stop or high-quality transit corridor (or on a lot with a multifamily, multistory dwelling).
  • 20 ft — the near-transit 18 ft plus 2 ft to match the primary dwelling's roof pitch. (There is no standalone 20 ft tier in the statute; 20 = 18 + 2.)
The Culver City break. CCMC 17.400.095.E.2.b.i sets the maximum at 30 ft for a detached ADU on a lot with an existing or proposed multifamily, multistory dwelling. Build the two SB9 units as a two-story duplex and the lot qualifies — so the back ADUs are not stuck at 16–20 ft. (State law only floors this at 18 ft; Culver City's 30 ft is a local upgrade — worth confirming on your parcel.) Extra height does not raise the floor-area cap — a taller detached ADU is still bound by the 1,200 sf maximum.

An attached ADU may reach 25 ft (or the R1 house limit, whichever is lower); the SB9 main dwellings follow R1 height — 27 ft flat, 30 ft with a roof pitched 3:12 or steeper.

4. The two SB9 + ADU combinations

The reason this works without a lot split is one line of the statute. GC 65852.21(j) says a city need not allow ADUs only when a parcel uses both the two-unit development and the urban lot split. Skip the lot split and the city must still permit your ADUs — so the two SB9 units and the ADUs stack. Two ways to combine them:

Path A — a duplex (the two units are attached)

A two-unit development built as a duplex makes the lot a proposed multiple-family dwelling, which allows up to two detached ADUs (CCMC 17.400.095.E.2.b.iii; GC 66323(a)(4)) — each a whole-unit FAR-free exemption unit (bankable 800 sf, larger on the upside), up to 30 ft on a multistory-MF lot. This is Strategy 1.

Path B — two detached one-family dwellings

Two detached houses (an existing or new main dwelling plus a second SB9 main dwelling) use the single-family ADU menu: one ADU plus one JADU. This is Strategy 2 — we use an attached ADU + a detached ADU to keep all four units full-size (with the JADU as the fallback fourth unit).

Confirm the ADU count — this is the #1 variable. Reaching four units assumes the City grants the full ADU allowance on top of the two SB9 units. SB9 two-unit developments can carry their own ADU-count limits, and the application of the multifamily ADU rules to a proposed (not existing) duplex is contestable — a conservative City could limit the lot to a single ADU. Verify the count with Culver City Planning before committing to a four-unit pro forma.

5. Strategy 1: Duplex in front, two ADUs in back

  • Make the front a legal SB9 duplex (two attached units that meet R1 standards). Both duplex units count toward FAR and share the 0.45 budget; they get full R1 height, 27–30 ft.
  • The headline: the duplex makes the lot multifamily, so the two detached back ADUs are each whole-unit FAR-free under the multifamily exemption (GC 66323(a)(4)) — bankable 800 sf each, larger on the upside — and because the duplex is a multistory multifamily dwelling, each can rise to up to 30 ft. Two fully-free detached units is why this is the maximum-square-footage play.
  • The binding limits on the back ADUs are height, the 4 ft side/rear setbacks, and the R1 side encroachment plane. R1 has no lot-coverage cap.
  • Four units: the two duplex units up front, plus the two detached ADUs. Zero parking on the ADUs; the SB9 units need one space each unless near transit or a car-share.

6. Strategy 2: Two detached main dwellings + attached ADU + detached ADU

  • Use the existing house or a new build as main dwelling one, and add a detached second main dwelling in the rear (a real two- to three-story R1 house, 27–30 ft). Both main dwellings share the 0.45 FAR budget. Keep detached dwellings at least 8 ft apart (CCMC 17.210.020).
  • One dwelling carries an attached ADU (up to 50% of that house; the City must allow at least 850 sf / 1,000 sf). It shares the house's height — but a new attached ADU is not a state-exemption unit, so its full floor area counts toward the 0.45 FAR, competing with the two houses for the budget.
  • Plus one detached ADU: an 800 sf State ADU that is whole-unit FAR-free (the usual pick). Build it larger and it stops being exempt — a 1,200 sf detached ADU counts in full, which is costly on a flat 0.45 budget — so keep it at 800 sf.
  • Four units: the two main dwellings, the attached ADU, and the detached ADU. If the City limits a single-family lot to one ADU, use a JADU (500 sf, inside a house, owner-occupancy) as the fourth unit instead.

8. Culver City's residential zones (how R1 compares)

Every lot in this playbook is R1 (Single-Family Residential). Unlike the City of Los Angeles, Culver City has no lettered/numbered R1 variation zones — R1 is a single set of standards with a flat 0.45 FAR. SB9 does not apply in the multifamily zones (they already allow multiple units); it applies in R1 and R2.

9. The other standards that shape a design

Setbacks

New SB9 units and ADUs need no more than 4 ft at the side and rear (ADUs may drop to 2 ft against a street, alley, or mixed-use zone). An existing main dwelling you keep retains its setbacks. The R1 front yard is 20 ft (25 ft for second-story massing over 18 ft). Keep detached dwellings 8 ft apart, and 5 ft between a primary and an ADU.

Parking

ADUs and JADUs require zero parking in Culver City (CCMC 17.400.095.I). SB9 units require zero within a half-mile of transit or one block of a car-share; otherwise about one space per unit. Tandem and uncovered spaces are allowed.

The R1 side encroachment plane

What actually limits the upper massing on an R1 lot is not lot coverage — R1 has none — it is the side setback plane: above 18 ft, a 1:1 (45°) plane rises and angles inward from each interior side lot line, so upper floors step back as the building climbs (CCMC 17.210.020). With the 27/30 ft height limit and the 0.45 FAR, that plane is the real shape constraint.

Objective design standards

SB9 two-unit developments are ministerial (no hearing, no CEQA) but must meet the city's Citywide Residential and Mixed-Use Objective Design Standards and the subdivision rules in CCMC Chapter 15.10 (Ord. 2025-007). ADUs and JADUs are exempt from those objective design standards (CCMC 17.400.095.N.2).

Eligibility gates (both strategies)

SB9 does not apply to a parcel in a historic district or on the historic register, in a very-high fire or flood hazard area, on protected habitat/wetlands, or to housing that is rent-restricted, income-restricted, or was tenant-occupied in the last three years (no more than 25% of exterior walls may be demolished unless the site was vacant). A two-unit development with no lot split carries no owner-occupancy requirement — that condition (a 3-year affidavit) only attaches to an urban lot split.

Overall takeaway

  • FAR is all-or-nothing per ADU. An ADU is either a whole-unit state exemption (FAR-free) or it counts in full — there is no "first 800 sf free" credit in Culver City. Grow a single-family detached ADU past 800 sf and the entire unit lands on the 0.45 budget. Bank 800 sf per exemption ADU; treat anything larger as needing City confirmation.
  • Strategy 1 (duplex + 2 detached ADUs) is the maximum-square-footage play. Both extra units are detached and use the multifamily exemption, so both stay whole-unit FAR-free (bankable 800 sf each, larger on the upside) and can reach up to 30 ft. On a 7,500 sf lot: ~4,975 sf bankable, ~5,775 sf upside across four units.
  • Strategy 2 (two houses + attached + detached ADU) is the full-height-houses play, but its total is smaller (~4,175 sf on a 7,500 sf lot): only the detached 800 sf ADU is FAR-free, while the attached ADU counts against the same 0.45 budget as the two houses.
  • Two things to confirm with the City before a four-unit pro forma: (1) the ADU count allowed on an SB9 lot, and (2) the qualifying exempt size of the duplex-back ADUs above 800 sf. No detached ADU can ever exceed 1,200 sf.
